# 🪵 Timber Takeoff Converter: Your Easy Guide

Hello there! Welcome to the **Timber Takeoff Converter**. 

Think of this program as your smart digital assistant for building projects. Whether you're working with floor joists, wall studs, or complex roof trusses, this tool is here to help you turn those messy PDF plans and Excel sheets into neat, organized lists that are ready for your workshop or supplier.

Instead of spending hours manually typing out measurements and looking up product codes, you can let our AI do the heavy lifting. This manual will walk you through everything you need to know in plain, simple English.

---

## 🌟 What Can This App Do For You?

### 🧠 It "Reads" Your Documents
Imagine having a helper who can look at a blueprint and instantly write down every piece of wood mentioned. That’s what our **Intelligent Analysis** does. It looks at your PDFs and pulls out the items, measurements, and quantities so you don’t have to.

### 📋 It Organizes Everything into a Simple Table
Once the app reads your files, it puts everything into a table that looks and feels just like Excel. You can click around, change numbers, and add notes easily.

### 🔗 It Connects to Your Catalog
No more guessing product codes. The app can look at your list of wood and automatically find the exact match in your company's product catalog (we call this **Catalog Mapping**). It even brings in the latest prices!

### 🔍 It Helps You Double-Check (Verification)
Sometimes you want to see exactly where a piece of information came from in the original document. Our **Verification View** lets you see your document and your data table side-by-side. When you click a row, it even highlights the part of the document it came from!

### 📐 It Helps You Save Wood (and Money)
Our **Cutting List Optimizer** is like a puzzle solver. It looks at all the pieces you need and figures out the smartest way to cut them from your long stock timber so you have as little waste as possible.

### 📂 It Keeps Your Projects Organized
Every job you do is saved in your **Job History**. You can group them into folders (like by the builder's name or the site location) so you can find them again in seconds.

## 🚀 How to Get Started

### 1. Bringing in Your Files
When you first open the app, you'll see a big friendly box that says "Upload Takeoff Files".

1.  **Choose a "Profile"**: This is just a way of telling the app what kind of file you're giving it. 
    *   **Standard PDF**: Usually the best bet for most blueprints.
    *   **Revit CSV Export**: Specialized for structured exports from Revit/CAD software.
2.  **Turn on "Smart Scanner" (Recommended)**: If your document is particularly messy or complicated, turn this on. It gives the app a little "extra brainpower" to understand it.
3.  **Upload Your File**: You can just drag your PDF or Excel file right onto the screen, or click the box to find it on your computer.

### 2. Picking Your Pages
If your PDF has many pages, a window will pop up asking which ones you want to process. Just select the pages that have your timber lists and click "Start". You'll see a progress bar move as the app "reads" the document.

## 🛠️ Navigating the Screen

### The Sidebar (Your Remote Control)
On the left side of the screen, you have your main navigation icons:
*   **🏠 New Job**: Takes you back to the start where you upload new files.
*   **📜 History**: This is your digital filing cabinet where all your past jobs live.
*   **📦 Catalog**: Where you manage the list of products your company sells and their prices.
*   **📈 Optimizer**: The place to go when you want to see the best way to cut your timber to avoid waste.
*   **📊 Report**: See the big picture with clear charts and summaries of your materials.
*   **⚙️ Settings**: Where you can change things like your company name, logo, and the "look" of the app.

### The Takeoff Table Toolbar (Your Toolbox)
When you're looking at a list of items, you'll see these buttons at the top:
*   **➕ Add Item**: Need to add something manually? Click this to pop in a new row.
*   **↩️ Undo / ↪️ Redo**: Made a mistake? Don't sweat it. These buttons let you hop back and forth through your changes.
*   **📊 Stats**: This toggles a set of cards that show you the big picture—like total length of timber or how much waste you have.
*   **🔍 Verify**: Opens the Side-by-Side verification view to check your data against the original document.
*   **📝 Notes**: Opens a little side panel where you can jot down important details about the job.
*   **💾 Export**: When you're finished, click this to save your list as a file you can open in Excel or send to a customer.

## 📝 Working With Your List

### Understanding the Colors
Sometimes you'll see cells in the table glow **Yellow or Orange**. This is just the app saying, "I'm not 100% sure about this one; could you take a quick look?" It’s always a good idea to double-check those cells.

### Connecting to the Catalog
To make your list professional, you'll want to connect your items to your real product catalog. 
1.  Click on the **"Spruce Item No"** or **"Description"** column.
2.  A search box will appear. Start typing the name of the product (like "MGP10").
3.  Click the right product from the list. The table will automatically update itself with the correct code and price.

## 💾 Managing Your Past Jobs

The **History** view is where you manage everything you've worked on.

*   **Make it Tidy**: You can create **Folders** to group jobs together. Maybe make a folder for "Smith Constructions" or "Site A".
*   **Compare Jobs**: Want to see how a new version of a plan changed? Select two jobs and click **"Compare"**. It will show you exactly what's different.
*   **Keep Backups**: You can click **"Backup"** to download all your history to your computer for safe-keeping.

## ⌨️ Hand Shortcuts (For the Speed-Demons)
If you want to move quickly without reaching for your mouse, try these:
*   **J** or **Down Arrow**: Move to the next item in the list.
*   **K** or **Up Arrow**: Move back up to the previous item.
*   **Enter**: Finish editing a cell and move to the next.
*   **Ctrl + S**: A quick way to save and export your work.
*   **V**: Quickly open the Verification View.
*   **Alt + R**: Quickly trigger a **Reparse** of the current document.
*   **Ctrl + K** (or **Cmd + K**): Open the **Command Bar** to search for any action or feature in the app.

## 🏗️ The Command Bar (One Place for Everything)

Can't find a button? Press **Ctrl + K** to open the **Command Bar**. 

Think of this like a "Spotlight" search for the app. You can type things like "Verify" or "Optimizer" or "Add Row", and the app will instantly show you that action. It's the fastest way to navigate for power users!

---

## 🏎️ Changing Profiles & Reparsing

If the initial parsing of your timber list isn't quite right, you can easily change the "brain" (Profile) being used:

1.  **Select a Profile**: In the table header, click the **Estimator Profile** dropdown. You can choose from existing profiles or generate a new one with AI.
2.  **Auto-Reparse**: When you pick a new profile, the app will **automatically** re-read the document using those new rules.
3.  **Manual Reparse**: You can also click the **"Reparse"** button (circular arrow icon) in the table toolbar at any time to refresh the data from the source document.

---

## 🔍 Using the Verification View (Side-by-Side)

The Verification View is one of the most powerful parts of the app. It lets you "audit" the AI's work.

1.  **Open it**: Click the **"Verify"** button in the top toolbar or press **'V'** on your keyboard.
2.  **Split Screen**: You'll see your original document on the left and the data table on the right. You can drag the middle bar to resize them.
3.  **Smart Highlights**: When you click on a row in the table, the original document will automatically scroll and highlight the exact spot where that information was found.
4.  **Edit as You Go**: You can change any value in the table on the right while looking at the source on the left.
5.  **Close**: Click the **'X'** in the top corner to return to the main view.

### Smart Toolbars
On small screens, some non-essential buttons might hide themselves to keep things tidy. Don't worry—all the same powerful features are still there, just organized to fit your screen.

### Side-by-Side on Tablets
On tablets, the **Verification View** is a great way to check your work. You can easily switch between the document and the data list with a single tap, or use the split-screen layout in landscape mode!
